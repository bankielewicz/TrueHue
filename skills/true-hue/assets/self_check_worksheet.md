# Self-Check Worksheet (Optional)

Use this if the user wants a structured way to verify their photos before submitting. Send it as an alternative to just the text checklist in the intake template — some users prefer the structured format.

---

## Pre-Submission Self-Check

Copy this into a note app or print it, and check off each item. If you can't check an item, the photo probably needs to be retaken.

### Lighting

- [ ] Photos were taken in natural daylight (not indoor bulbs, not flash, not mixed)
- [ ] Daylight source was indirect (near a window, not in direct sunbeam)
- [ ] Time of day was 10am–4pm (not dawn/dusk with warm cast, not late afternoon golden hour)
- [ ] No lamps, screens, or other colored light sources in view

### Device settings

- [ ] Flash was OFF
- [ ] Beauty mode / portrait mode / skin smoothing was OFF
- [ ] No filters were applied (Instagram, Snapchat, etc.)
- [ ] HDR was on or off consistently (doesn't matter which, but should be consistent)
- [ ] Photographic Styles (iPhone) set to Standard
- [ ] Scene Optimizer / AI Photo (Samsung) turned off

### Face and grooming

- [ ] No makeup of any kind (including tinted moisturizer, lip balm with color, brow gel)
- [ ] Hair pulled completely back — no strands across face, ears visible
- [ ] No glasses (remove for eye close-up specifically)
- [ ] No colored contact lenses (remove if possible)
- [ ] Face is clean and dry (not sweaty, not freshly washed with redness)

### Clothing and setup

- [ ] Wearing plain white or medium gray shirt, OR
- [ ] Draped a neutral white/gray towel or sheet over shoulders
- [ ] No colored clothing visible within 6 inches of face
- [ ] Background is plain (wall, curtain, door) — not a colored or patterned backdrop

### White paper reference

- [ ] Used plain white printer/copier paper (not cream, not eggshell, not ivory)
- [ ] Paper was held at cheek level in shot 1
- [ ] Paper is large enough to read easily in the photo (at least palm-sized in frame)
- [ ] Paper is flat and not casting a shadow

### Five required photos

- [ ] Shot 1: Front of face with white paper reference
- [ ] Shot 2: Side profile of face
- [ ] Shot 3: Close-up of both eyes, open and looking at camera
- [ ] Shot 4: Inner wrist, palm-up, showing veins (if visible)
- [ ] Shot 5: Back of hand flat on sheet of white paper

### Questionnaire

- [ ] Natural hair color noted
- [ ] Current hair (colored or not, and if colored, what color)
- [ ] Sun response described
- [ ] Jewelry test noted (gold vs. silver vs. rose gold)
- [ ] Compliments list (specific colors, not generic)
- [ ] Fails list (specific colors, not generic)
- [ ] Eye color details including light-shift behavior
- [ ] Wrist vein color (or noted as "not visible")
- [ ] Heritage (optional)
- [ ] Dye history (if applicable)
- [ ] Context/goal for the analysis
- [ ] Grooming categories relevant to you selected in the questionnaire (see intake template, item 14)

### Final gate check

Before submitting:

- [ ] I reviewed my photos in full-screen mode on a large display, not just the camera preview
- [ ] The white paper in the reference shot appears neutral white (not yellow, blue, or pink)
- [ ] My face has even illumination (no half-shadowed face)
- [ ] I can clearly see iris detail in the eye close-up
- [ ] I am submitting all five photos, not just some

---

## If any item is unchecked

The analysis quality will suffer. Two options:

1. **Fix it and re-shoot.** Most fixes are 5-minute re-shoots. This is the higher-value path.
2. **Submit anyway and flag it.** Tell Claude what you couldn't fix — e.g., "My apartment only has south-facing windows, so lighting was a bit warmer than ideal." Claude will adjust confidence level and note the caveat in the deliverable.

The worst option is submitting without mentioning an issue and getting a falsely confident result. Better to flag problems than hide them.
