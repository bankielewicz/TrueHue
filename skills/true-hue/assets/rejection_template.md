# Photo Rejection Template

Use this scaffold when rejecting submitted photos. The goal is to be specific and actionable — not to shame the user, but to tell them exactly what to redo so they don't waste another round.

## Format

```
Thanks for submitting. I can't run a reliable analysis on these photos yet — here's what needs to be fixed before I continue.

**Issues found:**

[For each issue, state: which photo, what's wrong, why it matters, what to do]

**What to keep from this submission:**

[If any photos passed, note them so the user only has to reshoot the failures]

**What to retake:**

1. [Specific shot with specific fix]
2. [Specific shot with specific fix]

If you can reshoot in the next 24 hours while conditions are similar, that's ideal. Once I have clean inputs I can deliver the full analysis.
```

## Issue phrasings by failure type

**Warm indoor lighting:**
> Photo 1 shows a yellow/orange cast on the white paper reference — this tells me the light source was an incandescent or warm LED bulb, not daylight. Skin will read warmer than it truly is under this light. Please retake near a window during daylight hours with no lamps on.

**Flash fired:**
> Photo 2's EXIF data shows flash was triggered. Direct flash flattens skin tones and washes out undertone. Please retake with flash disabled in natural ambient light.

**Mixed lighting:**
> Photo 3 has two light sources — daylight from the left and a warm lamp from the right, visible in the shadow direction and color temperature split. Turn off all indoor lights and use daylight only.

**Makeup visible:**
> Photo 1 shows foundation and mascara. Even tinted moisturizer or BB cream changes the apparent skin tone significantly. Please wash your face and retake.

**Filter/beauty mode:**
> The skin in photo 1 is smoothed and the eyes appear enlarged — your camera's beauty or portrait mode is on. Please check your camera settings and disable any face enhancement, then retake.

**Hair not pulled back:**
> Hair is falling across the cheek in photo 2, which obscures the jawline reading and can reflect color onto the neck. Please pull hair fully behind the ears with a headband or clip and retake.

**Colored clothing:**
> A [color] shirt is visible in photo 1. This reflects onto your jaw and neck and skews the skin tone reading. Please change into a white or gray top (or drape a neutral towel over your shoulders) and retake.

**No white reference:**
> Photo 1 doesn't include a sheet of white printer paper next to the face. Without a known neutral I can't verify the white balance of your camera. Please hold a sheet of plain copier paper at cheek level in the front-of-face shot and retake.

**White paper reads off-neutral:**
> The white paper in photo 1 reads noticeably [yellow/blue/pink], which means your camera or the ambient light is pulling color into the image. The skin tone in this photo can't be trusted. Try: (a) different window, (b) different time of day, (c) camera white balance set to daylight or auto, (d) outdoors on an overcast day.

**Harsh shadow:**
> Photo 2 has a strong shadow across the right side of your face — the light source was too directional. Move to a spot where light wraps around the face more evenly (step back from the window, or stand in open shade).

**Missing shot:**
> I don't see the wrist vein close-up (shot 4). This is one of the undertone cross-checks and can't be substituted by other photos. Please add this one.

**Backlit:**
> Photo 1 has the light coming from behind you — your face is in shadow relative to the background. Turn around so you're facing the light source (but still indirect, not direct sun).

## Tone

Be direct but not condescending. The user probably did their best and didn't realize how sensitive this is to lighting. Frame it as "this is a known hard problem that human professionals also struggle with" rather than "you did this wrong."

Never reject silently and proceed anyway. If the photos aren't good enough, saying "here's my best guess" gives the user a false result that looks authoritative. The whole point of this skill is to refuse that pattern.
