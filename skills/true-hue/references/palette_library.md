# Palette Library — Hex Codes by Season

Validated color sets for each of the 12 seasons. Each palette is anchored by **LAB-validated core colors** from Hue Atlas (published reference with mathematical validation) and extended to a ~36-color working palette organized by wardrobe role.

**Validation methodology:**
- Core anchors (marked with ★) are drawn from Hue Atlas's LAB-validated palettes — these have been tested for temperature, saturation, and seasonal alignment
- Supporting colors are interpolated and extended using color theory principles consistent with the anchor character
- Descriptions and "why this fails" reasoning for avoid colors are synthesized from The Concept Wardrobe, Palette Path, and other published 12-season references

**Important:** Hex codes render slightly differently on every screen. Always caveat this in the deliverable. For shopping, users should verify items in natural daylight before committing. For truly professional-grade palette accuracy, in-person consultation with calibrated fabric swatches remains the gold standard.

## Adjustments for skin depth

Within any season, the same hex codes render differently against different skin depths. Guidance:

- **Fair skin in warm seasons** (Light Spring, Soft Autumn): can go slightly lighter and cleaner with mids; darkest darks may overwhelm
- **Deep skin in warm seasons** (Warm Autumn, Deep Autumn): can go richer and more saturated than the palette suggests; the "safe" mid-range may read too muted against highly melanated skin
- **Fair skin in cool seasons** (Light Summer, Cool Summer): can use icy pastels freely; saturated darks need care
- **Deep skin in cool seasons** (Deep Winter, Cool Winter): these seasons are well-suited to deep skin generally; lean into the saturated mids, especially jewel tones like emerald, sapphire, true red
- **Olive complexions** in any season: favor the greyed-down versions of palette colors; avoid pure pinks and pure yellows; greens that harmonize with olive cast (muted olive, sage) work better than clear greens (lime, kelly green)

## Usage notes

- These are starting-point palettes. Skilled analysis adjusts slightly based on the individual — a Soft Autumn with slightly cooler undertones might borrow a few Soft Summer colors.
- LAB-validated anchors (★) represent the mathematical core of each season — they're the most reliable colors in each palette.
- When the user shops, emphasize: verify items in natural light before committing.
- "Statement" colors are the highest-octane items — use in small doses or for occasions.
- For the "avoid" colors in the deliverable, pick 8-12 that are most commonly encountered in mainstream clothing rather than obscure ones. Explain *why* each one fails.
