# The 12-Season Color System — Reference

The 12-season system (Sci\ART and related frameworks) extends the classic 4-season system by adding a "flow" dimension between adjacent seasons. Each classic season has three sub-seasons that lean toward its neighbors.

## The flow chart

```
                    SPRING
       Light ←  Warm  →  Bright
         ↓                  ↓
      SUMMER             WINTER
    Light ← Soft       Deep → Cool
         ↓                  ↓
       AUTUMN             WINTER
    Soft  ← Warm  →  Deep
```

Each season has three variants. The middle variant (Warm Spring, Cool Summer, Warm Autumn, Cool Winter) is the "true" expression of the season. The outer variants blend toward the next season on the chart.

## Naming conventions — acknowledging variants

The 12-season system has multiple naming conventions across published sources. This skill standardizes on specific names but recognizes the following as equivalent:

| This skill uses | Also known as | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Deep Autumn | Dark Autumn | Same sub-season, same palette; Sci\ART systems use "Dark" more often than "Deep" |
| Deep Winter | Dark Winter | Same sub-season, same palette; used interchangeably in the industry |
| Warm Spring | True Spring | Considered the "archetype" Spring; some systems call it "Warm" others "True" |
| Warm Autumn | True Autumn | Same archetype relationship |
| Cool Summer | True Summer | Same archetype relationship |
| Cool Winter | True Winter | Same archetype relationship |

If a user arrives referring to themselves as a "Dark Autumn," this is the same as Deep Autumn. If they refer to "True Winter," this corresponds to Cool Winter. The skill treats them as interchangeable and uses the table above in its responses when naming variation comes up.

A known industry phenomenon: Soft Summer has been widely described as a "diagnostic dumping ground" — ambiguous cases often get assigned there by default. This skill pushes back against that pattern by requiring specific evidence for a Soft Summer reading (not just "the other options didn't seem right").

## The 12 seasons defined

### Spring family (all warm, all bright-ish)

**Light Spring** — Leans toward Summer
- Undertone: warm, leaning neutral
- Value: light
- Chroma: bright but delicate
- Contrast: low
- Typical coloring: light-value warm-leaning coloring with bright-but-delicate chroma and low overall contrast. Heritage examples: fair peach-skinned Northern European with golden-blonde hair and clear blue or green eyes; light-warm East Asian with honey-toned skin and light-warm brown hair; light-warm Latin American with peach-gold complexion and medium-light warm brown eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Warm Spring** — The true Spring
- Undertone: warm (golden)
- Value: medium-light
- Chroma: bright, clear
- Contrast: medium
- Typical coloring: clearly warm undertone across a range of skin depths, with bright clear chroma and medium contrast. Heritage examples: golden-peach Northern European with strawberry blonde or copper hair and warm green or warm hazel eyes; warm-golden South Asian with rich copper-warm complexion and warm-brown eyes; warm Latin American with honey-gold skin and warm amber or clear warm-brown eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Bright Spring** — Leans toward Winter
- Undertone: warm, leaning neutral
- Value: medium
- Chroma: very bright, vivid
- Contrast: medium-high
- Typical coloring: warm-neutral undertone at medium value with very bright vivid chroma and medium-high contrast — this is the most saturated of the Springs. Heritage examples: clear-complexioned East Asian with bright warm-black hair and vivid dark eyes; warm-bright Latin American with clear warm complexion and bright deep brown or vivid hazel eyes; clear-complexioned Northern European with crisp warm complexion and vivid blue, green, or hazel eyes. Bright Spring occurs across East Asian, Latin American, South Asian, and European heritages alike — the common thread is the clear, vivid quality, not any specific ethnic default. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

### Summer family (all cool, all muted)

**Light Summer** — Leans toward Spring
- Undertone: cool, leaning neutral
- Value: light
- Chroma: muted but not dusty
- Contrast: low
- Typical coloring: cool-leaning undertone at light value with muted-but-not-dusty chroma and low contrast. Heritage examples: fair cool-skinned Northern European with ash blonde or light ash brown hair and soft grey-blue or soft hazel eyes; light-cool East Asian with cool-beige complexion and soft cool-brown eyes; light-cool mixed-heritage user with cool fair-medium skin and cool hazel or soft cool-green eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Cool Summer** — The true Summer
- Undertone: cool
- Value: medium-light
- Chroma: muted, soft
- Contrast: low-medium
- Typical coloring: clearly cool undertone at medium-light value with soft muted chroma and low-medium contrast — this season is not race-specific. Heritage examples: cool pink-beige Northern European with ash-brown hair and cool blue or cool green eyes; cool medium-skinned East or Southeast Asian with cool-undertone medium complexion and cool dark brown eyes; cool-toned mixed-heritage user with cool fair or cool medium-light skin and cool grey or cool hazel eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Soft Summer** — Leans toward Autumn
- Undertone: cool, leaning neutral
- Value: medium
- Chroma: very muted, dusty
- Contrast: low
- Typical coloring: cool-neutral undertone (often with slight olive cast) at medium value with very muted dusty chroma and low contrast — the overall impression is soft and understated. Heritage examples: olive-cool Mediterranean with mousy or ash brown hair and soft hazel or dusty blue-green eyes; mousy-neutral Northern or Eastern European with ash brown hair and soft cool-hazel eyes; olive-cool South Asian or Middle Eastern with medium cool-olive complexion and soft cool-brown eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

### Autumn family (all warm, all muted)

**Soft Autumn** — Leans toward Summer
- Undertone: warm, leaning neutral
- Value: medium
- Chroma: muted
- Contrast: low-medium
- Typical coloring: warm-neutral undertone (often olive-warm) at medium value with muted chroma and low-medium contrast. Heritage examples: warm-beige Northern or Western European with warm dusty brown or dark blonde hair and warm hazel eyes; olive-warm Mediterranean with warm-medium complexion and soft warm-brown eyes; warm-neutral Latin American or South Asian with medium warm-olive complexion and warm hazel or soft warm-brown eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Warm Autumn** — The true Autumn
- Undertone: warm (golden/copper)
- Value: medium-deep
- Chroma: muted but rich
- Contrast: medium
- Typical coloring: clearly warm undertone at medium-deep value with muted-but-rich chroma and medium contrast. Heritage examples: warm-medium West African with richly pigmented warm complexion and warm-brown or amber eyes; warm-golden Mediterranean with copper, auburn, or warm chestnut hair and warm-brown eyes; warm-rich South Asian with deep warm-gold complexion and warm dark brown eyes; warm Middle Eastern with olive-warm complexion and warm dark eyes; warm Northern European with deep auburn or warm chestnut hair. Warm Autumn shows up across the full range of skin depths whenever warmth and muted richness converge. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Deep Autumn** — Leans toward Winter
- Undertone: warm, leaning neutral
- Value: deep
- Chroma: muted but saturated
- Contrast: medium-high
- Typical coloring: warm-neutral undertone at deep value with muted-but-saturated chroma and medium-high contrast — Deep Autumn prominently includes deeply pigmented skin with warm undertones. Heritage examples: richly pigmented West African or Afro-Caribbean with deep warm complexion and warm dark-brown or black-brown eyes; deep warm-toned South Asian with richly pigmented complexion and dark warm-brown eyes; deep warm Mediterranean or Middle Eastern with dark warm-brown hair and warm dark eyes; deep Latin American or Indigenous American with warm deep complexion and dark warm-brown eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

### Winter family (all cool, all bright)

**Deep Winter** — Leans toward Autumn
- Undertone: cool, leaning neutral
- Value: deep
- Chroma: bright
- Contrast: high
- Typical coloring: cool-neutral undertone at deep value with bright chroma and high contrast — Deep Winter prominently includes deeply pigmented skin with cool-neutral undertones, and occurs across African, South Asian, East Asian, Mediterranean, and Northern European dark-cool heritages. Heritage examples: richly pigmented West African or Afro-Caribbean with cool-neutral deep complexion and deep cool-brown or near-black eyes; deep cool-toned South Asian with cool-undertoned deep complexion and dark cool eyes; deep East Asian with cool-undertoned complexion and dark cool eyes; cool-toned Mediterranean with dark cool-brown hair and deep cool eyes; cool Northern European with very dark brown or near-black hair and dark cool eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Cool Winter** — The true Winter
- Undertone: cool
- Value: medium-deep
- Chroma: bright, icy
- Contrast: high
- Typical coloring: clearly cool undertone at medium-deep value with bright icy chroma and high contrast — Cool Winter is not a race-specific category. Heritage examples: cool porcelain Northern European with ash-brown to near-black hair and cool blue or cool grey eyes; cool medium-skinned East Asian with cool-undertone medium complexion and cool dark eyes; cool-undertoned South Asian with cool medium or cool deep complexion and cool dark eyes; cool-toned mixed-heritage user with cool fair-medium skin and cool grey, blue, or dark eyes. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

**Bright Winter** — Leans toward Spring
- Undertone: cool, leaning neutral
- Value: medium-deep
- Chroma: very bright, vivid
- Contrast: very high
- Typical coloring: cool-neutral undertone at medium-deep value with very bright vivid chroma and the most dramatic contrast of all seasons. Heritage examples: clear cool Northern European with dark brown or near-black hair and vivid clear blue, green, or hazel eyes; clear-complexioned East Asian with bright dark hair and vivid cool-dark eyes; clear-skinned Latin American with cool complexion and vivid dark or bright hazel eyes; clear cool South Asian with cool-undertoned complexion and vivid dark eyes. Bright Winter shows up across multiple heritages wherever high contrast and vivid chroma converge on a cool base. The underlying dimensions (undertone + value + chroma + contrast) are what matter, not specific ethnic features.

## Adjacency map (for draping comparison)

When concluding a season, you MUST justify why not the two adjacent seasons.

| If you concluded... | Adjacent 1 | Adjacent 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Light Spring | Light Summer | Warm Spring |
| Warm Spring | Light Spring | Bright Spring |
| Bright Spring | Warm Spring | Bright Winter |
| Light Summer | Light Spring | Cool Summer |
| Cool Summer | Light Summer | Soft Summer |
| Soft Summer | Cool Summer | Soft Autumn |
| Soft Autumn | Soft Summer | Warm Autumn |
| Warm Autumn | Soft Autumn | Deep Autumn |
| Deep Autumn | Warm Autumn | Deep Winter |
| Deep Winter | Deep Autumn | Cool Winter |
| Cool Winter | Deep Winter | Bright Winter |
| Bright Winter | Cool Winter | Bright Spring |
